Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

A man with big dreams.

Timeline of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam's Life and Achievements

1931 – Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu

Born on October 15, 1931, in a small town on the southern tip of India

His parents, Zainulabiddin and Ashiamma, instilled values of hard work and humility.

1954 – Graduation in Physics

Completed his graduation in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.

Went on to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), where he developed a keen interest in aeronautics.

1954 – Graduation in Physics

Completed his graduation in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.

Went on to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), where he developed a keen interest in aeronautics

1960s – Starting Career in ISRO

Joined the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR) in 1962, working under Dr. Vikram Sarabhai.

Played a crucial role in India's first satellite launch, SLV-3, which successfully placed the Aryabhata

1983 – Director of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP)

Led the development of key missiles like Prithvi, Agni, and Akash.

His leadership helped India become self-reliant in missile technology.

1992-1999 – Scientific Advisor to the Defence Minister

Played a pivotal role in India's nuclear weapons program.

Oversaw Pokhran-II, a series of successful nuclear tests in 1998, which led to India being recognized as a nuclear power.

2002-2007 – President of India

Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002.

Focused on youth empowerment, science, technology, and national development.

Became affectionately known as the "People's President" for his accessibility and approachability.

2006 – Vision 2020

Dr. Kalam articulated his vision for India's future in the form of Vision 2020, which aimed to make India a developed nation.

Emphasized the importance of education, innovation, and entrepreneurship.

2015 – Passing Away

On July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong, Dr. Kalam passed away.

He collapsed during the lecture, leaving behind a legacy of wisdom, innovation, and inspiration.
